About Guido Spanò

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at University College London.

I am a macroeconomist with research interests in monetary economics, macro-finance and banking. My work studies how monetary policy is transmitted through banks, with a particular focus on interest-rate pass-through, bank funding structure and reserve regimes.

Working papers

Banks’ Funding Structure and Pass-Through in the Euro Area

with Juan Manuel Figueres

Abstract. This paper studies how banks’ funding structures shape the transmission of monetary-policy rates to deposit and lending rates in the euro area. Using bank-level interest-rate and balance-sheet data, we document faster pass-through to lending rates than to household deposit rates and substantial heterogeneity across banks. Banks relying more on market-based funding adjust their rates more strongly, while more stable funding structures are associated with weaker pass-through.

Ample Reserves and Deposit Pass-Through

Abstract. This paper studies how the quantity of central-bank reserves affects the transmission of policy rates to bank deposit rates. A model combining deposit market power with stochastic reserve shortfalls and collateralised central-bank borrowing characterises pass-through under scarce- and ample-reserve regimes and evaluates how balance-sheet policies change banks’ rate-setting incentives.

with Luca Citino, Marta Morazzoni and Stefano Pietrosanti

We study why senior leadership is especially prevalent among Italian firms and how managerial age and tenure interact with access to credit, productivity and the firm-size distribution.
